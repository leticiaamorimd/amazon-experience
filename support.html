Amazon original groups are endangered

The Amazon is the ancestral home of 1 million Indigenous people.
They are divided into about 400 tribes, each with its own language, culture and territory.

We live in times when environmental destruction and the terrible impact of covid-19, particularly among indigenous peoples, give terms such as genocide and ecocide special prominence. Understanding its trajectory is fundamental for reevaluating a certain notion of progress – much mobilized in periods such as the military dictatorship and today – which is based on the destruction of landscapes and bodies.
